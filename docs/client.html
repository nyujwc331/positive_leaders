Building a client base

  • Idea is you cast a very wide net and to say to people - I am doing this now I would love any referrals possible that you could send my way.
    • Make a list of 10 people you know, individuals that you could conceivably coach directly or they might be people who know people you can coach.
    • Speak with each of these people on your list.

      • Things you can say:

        Explain your coaching training and what coaching has to offer, and the kind of clients you’re seeking to serve. Do you know anyone who would fit this description? Ask if they know someone who might be interested in a complimentary call.

        We are both going to benefit here, I am working towards my certification. The more hours I put in that are paid the better and the closer I get to that goal. In exchange, I can offer a lower rate than I will be charging once I am certified.

        I am not charging full fees yet because I want this to be beneficial to others as much as it is to me in terms of my learning, growth, and progress towards certification. But, I have no question that I will be able to do some things for people that will help move them forward.

        I am in this program and it is really exciting, and I am learning such terrific stuff. I really enjoy seeing what a difference it can make in people’s lives and I would love to be able to offer that. Do you know anyone who might be interested?

        Hey, I am launching this coaching company. I am really excited about it and have so much enthusiasm for this. It would be great if I were able to offer this service to people that you know who might benefit.

        Hey, I am open for business if you know someone who is interested send them my way.

      • DON’T say:

        Don’t ever sound like you need clients.Focus on serving the client, not on “needing” a client. Think about using your signature strengths to tell people what is unique about you as a coach.

    • Encourage clients to refer others to you.

      I am still open for clients so if you are comfortable, please feel free to refer anyone who might be interested.

    • Talk about your coaching every chance you get.

      • There are ways to do it where you don’t make people run away everytime they see you coming. There are ways to bring it up just as a natural part of your conversation.

        Simply ask if they have a 5 year plan.

    • Online
      • Your own website.

      • Some websites you can list yourself as a coach and post a profile.

        Psychology today

      • Social media

        Facebook

        LinkedIn - If you have premium membership, you can list yourself as a coach or expert.

    • Coaching firms

      Better up - They require that you are certified or have achieved a certain part of that process before you connect with them. You are being paid far less than you can charge privately.

      PLUMA - Require ~10 years in management.

Frequency & Time

  • Entirely the client’s determination.
    • Whatever the client feels is going to serve his or her needs best.
    • Most often I have people finish the session and tell me when they would like to meet again, and we set a mutually agreeable time for our next session.
    • Depending on how urgently you want to get going on this thing people will gain more traction or momentum if they meet weekly.

Pricing

  • What does your market support?
  • Sliding scale

    • If you are going on the basis of a sliding scale, Ben Dean’s personal recommendation is to limit that to a 3 month agreement. I can offer you a sliding scale for you at which time we will take stock and see where things are financially. At the 3 month mark, let’s recap here, where we have been, what is going on, where we are headed, and to determine how things are working for you financially.

      This is what I am charging until I get capped out with so many clients.

      This is what I am charging until I reach a certain number of hours.

      Gradual increase where you or they determine the price.

      Certified so now I am full price.

      Unfortunately, I am now not in a position to continue on a sliding scale, my rates are these.

    • You can determine the price. When you take stock, you should have the new price in your head already. Then it is up to the person to continue or not. You would want to have a more gradual increase. For example, maybe it is every 3 months or every month that your prices go up.

    • They can determine the price. I am going to be 100% in. I am going to be here as if you are paying me my full fee. I need you to show up that way too. You decide what you can afford. Figure out what you can afford a month - what discretionary amount you have per month in your budget. Then decide, how often you like to meet over a month and then do the math. I don’t want to make you feel uncomfortable. I just want to be very sure that this is not going to cause any kind of distress for you financially, especially if you are gaining lots and lots of benefit from it and you would like this to continue over a longer period of time.

      Showed clients I wasn’t doing it for the money and that I trusted them to be honest. Inevitably, people would want to offer me more than I had the sense they could really afford because anything less was insulting.

  • Your fees are any number you can get to come out of your mouth.

    I charge anywhere from $2 to $200 an hr.

  • Introductory fee

    I invited people to purchase a package of 4 sessions (4 hours) $100. The concern is that I had people pay for that set of 4 sessions and only come for 2. I felt a moral obligation to try and get that person’s money back to them.

    The ballpark for new coaches is to hold weekly sessions for about $200 per four sessions of 30 – 45 minutes each.

  • Charge per session that is conducted- Irene’s personal preference.

  • Bartering of services

    I bartered my gym membership for one year. I was willing to coach the owner and any of his staff that he wanted to put forward, til I had accrued the hours that we felt would be equivalent to my membership for that year.

    Offer to coach the person who designs your logo.

  • Negotiate a deal

    I will certainly agree to a sliding scale rate until you find a job and then once that is established we will renegotiate.

    Offer a lesser fee for every-other-week coaching. For instance, $400 per month for every week or $250 per month for every other week.

    Offer pairs coaching for clients working on similar goals. Ex: You may coach two clients working to earn their certification. They meet every week and pay a total of $450, which is $225 each for monthly coaching.

    Offer a group experience if available and appropriate for the client. Group fees are often “Twice the time for ½ the price.” Ex: Coach’s monthly fee for 30 minutes is $300. Weekly one-hour group coaching, twice a month is $150.

    If the client cannot afford the lower fee acceptable to the coach, it is helpful for the coach to refer the client to other coaches who might have a lower sliding scale.
