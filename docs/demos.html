    How are we at achieving you agenda for today?

    What form of accountability would you like around this?

    How would you like to keep yourself accountable?

    Would it help to spend time talking about accountability at this point?

    We never assume that we are going to keep clients accountable. We empower clients to keep themselves accountable. We invite them to consider what they would like their accountability would look like or what form of accountability if any would you like to talk about. You will be expected though to do it at the end of the session. This is one of the things you can kind of flex on as you get into private practice and you are past certification. But it is good practice to get into asking for accountability at the end of every session.

  • Wrap up

    Did you get what you needed today?

    How did we do at accomplishing the agenda you brought to today’s session?

6.1 WIA

Irene Nizzero, MCP 236, March 4, 2020 @ 4 PM - 5:30 PM

  • Do you mind sharing your top 5 strengths?

Comment on them and how they are unique or interesting. Thank the person for sharing.

  • Any reactions to the results?

Did they sound like you, not like you, were you surprised by any of the top strengths, or how they were rank ordered? Did you have the experience of this got me?

  • If not, what would have been different?

Explanation for when people think they should have different strengths in their top 5. The top 5 signature strengths are our default behaviors or patterns. You can consider all 5 your top 1, and they can be juggled in any way and are consider interchangeable. The rest of your strengths come in clusters, and when they come in at the same value they are list aphabetically. Generally speaking 1 to 10 stay fairly stable, but something might slip from 3 to 7 or creep up from 6 to 4. However, you are not likely to see a lot of movement, unless there is a concerted effort to make some changes in your lower strengths.

Close race

Recommend using a Success/Strengths Journal

The goal is NOT to increase the lower strengths or lesser strengths. It is to put yourself into a position where you use your top strengths more thus leading to more engagement/fulfillment in your life. None of the strengths are weaknesses. You can spend all kinds of time trying to work on those lower strengths if you want. However, if you were to spend as much time increasing or focusing on your default strengths, you would actually get more bang for your buck. You would yield better, enduring, and more impactful results if you focus on your strengths. They are your default, who you naturally are. It is okay to have lower strengths, you can’t be good at everything.

  • Do you think that this might be related to an overuse/underuse of your strengths?

  • Which of your top 5 strengths could help with this situation?

Well, how could kindness factor in here, or how could your strength of kindness be of value in this situation. I might pick one, but generally we leave that to the client. More generally we ask which of your top 5 strengths could add something here or make a difference.

  • Can you somehow find a new way to use a strength?

  • Can the use of a strength help regulate another strength or lesser strength?

6.5 Stages of Change

Based on Prochaska

Irene Nizzero, MCP 236, April 22, 2020 @ 4 PM - 5:30 PM

Prochaska’s basic premise is that if you don’t catch a person when they are ready for, at the right stage for readiness for change, you are likely to fail. You are trying to invite a person to move forward on something that they are simply are not ready move forward on. The first four stages of change are the ones that are the most connected to coaching.

  • Precontemplation

    • Raise client’s awareness of behaviors they want to change.

A person in precontemplation has this vague sense that change may be necessary, but is not self aware of the issue at hand. A phrase that is a tip off, is “I guess I should be thinking about this.” That is, they haven’t even begun to think about this. This person is probably going to be very resistant to change. This is a person who has up until this point been pretty content in not knowing that there was ever something that needed changing. There is also a level of denial going on with this person. Yet, they may bring it to you for coaching.

Explore with that person what part of that whole directive is starting to feeling important to them.

  • Talk with the client in a very frank way to say we can’t deal with all of that so what part of that is feeling like the priority for you?

In order to really help a client in this stage, open up possibilities with this client.

Start to envision with the client what would a better option for them, or how could this be improved. Starting to really explore what will that give the client and what is the importance of that to the client.

  • What would you like as your goal here if you don’t want to go grab takeout everyday for lunch; what would you like instead?

The client will then start to envision, I am going to save money; I am going to have better control over what goes into my body; and why is that important. Always drilling down to why this might start to sound like a good idea for the client. But remember that however you explore this with a client, you are looking to open up possibilities, i.e. create possibilities with the person as opposed to shutting down options. That makes it more enticing to move in that direction.

  • Are you curious about …

  • Contemplation

    • Monitor the problem behavior and client’s attitudes about it.
    • Create list of pros and cons for the behavior.
    • Support groups outside of coaching.
    • Journaling thoughts, feelings, plans, etc.
    • Coaching occurs often in this stage!

The person has started to acknowledge and think if I really started to look at this and take control over it, I bet I could have a lot more energy. I bet this would be a benefit to me on a few levels. The client is starting to at least see some potential for moving in the direction. However, they are still fence sitting on the issue. They understand there is an issue here and there could be some room for improving the situation but still a bit unsure about what kind of commitment is this going to take from them and if they are up for it. They are thinking I guess I should do this at some point, but that at some point phrase becomes the problem.

  • You want to get them to start imagining what change might look like?
  • What happens if you do nothing?

Help the client crystalize in his or her mind why this is a great thing to do and what good could come of this.

  • What do you stand to gain by making these changes?

You are also managing their inner critic that might be saying things like well I don’t know I have tried sort of doing this sort of thing before.

May want to have the client consider enlisting the support of others around them.

  • What do you risk if you don’t change?
  • If you don’t make this change could it be so bad?

  • How itis preventing your progress?

  • Preparation and Planning

    • Help client build on their plans.
    • Ask them how they will feel when an action is completed.
    • Ask them what resources they need for success.
    • Do they have concrete dates set?
    • Who have they shared their plans with?
    • Coaching occurs often in this stage!

Have the client imagine at least sort of setting things up so they are ready to begin the process. It is the idea of setting the person up for success. If a client jumps this step it often will come back in a coaching session that the person tried to go from contemplation to action, without the preparation between and failed. It is about setting up the resource, both in terms of people and supplies, things, systems that are necessary to make this work.

  • What do we need to do to get you ready to start…?
  • What do you need to set yourself up for success?
  • What do you need to change in your environment?
  • What do you need to do differently in terms of habits?
  • How might you want to change some of those habit?
  • What patterns in your day might impact on these decisions or plans that you have?
  • What patterns might be part of your morning, evening habits, that could be contributing here?
  • What behaviors do you find yourself engaging in regularly?

Contemplation and planning and preparation are the stages that probably the bulk of the coaching will happen.

  • Action

    • Acknowledgement: recognize client’s strengths being applied.
    • Celebration: praise the client for their progress, however small, and for their new beliefs.

Really crucial to help the client see before getting into action, the desired future state. It is also really critical that the person not bite off more than they can actuall do. There are ways to have very direct communication around that to ensure that what clients have set up for themselves is a realistic goal.

  • What might you anticipate as barriers to this happening for you?
  • Could you imagine barriers coming up for you this week that you would want to plan for, and what might those be?
  • What barriers might come up around that this week?

In asking these types of questions, chances are they will start to think through it. The client will usually make more reasonable goals. However, if not as coach you are going with it and support them.

They may not get it perfect the first time. Acknowledge every little bit of their success during this stage. Acknowledge their enthusiasm, the energy for the idea, their desire for change, these are all good things. They view it as positive so you enforcing that yes it is positive is NOT a suggestion.

Leave it to the client. It is always up to the client to bring progress or talk about progress with you as opposed to you raising it with them.

  • Would it be helpful to talk about this a bit?

Let them decide whether or not they want to go anywhere with that.
